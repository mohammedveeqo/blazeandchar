The Story

Slow-marinated British topside in a handcrafted tandoori blend — Kashmiri chilli, smoked paprika, tamarind — then slow-dried at 70°C for bold, clean flavour. One batch a day, by hand, in Wales.

"No soy. No MSG. No artificial preservatives."

This version implements every must-fix from your labelling review. A few items remain as placeholders because they depend on data I don't have (year, address, lab results, real barcode). Swap them before print.

  • "Born, reared & slaughtered UK" replaces "British sourced".
  • Ingredients rewritten to match your actual recipe — tamarind water, tomato purée, lemon juice, ACV, turmeric, kasuri methi, black cardamom added; yoghurt, cassia, rapeseed oil removed.
  • Milk allergen removed (no yoghurt). Only mustard remains as a regulated allergen, emphasised inline as MUSTARD powder and repeated in the allergen line.

  • "e" mark dropped from net weight.

  • Barcode shows placeholder label — no invented EAN-13 numbers.
  • Wales mark is now a tiny outline silhouette of Wales — not a green circle implying a cert scheme.

You still need to confirm before print

  • Est. year (if keeping) — replace [YYYY] in the back maker block only. Consider dropping entirely for pre-launch.
  • Maker address — replace [Maker address, Wales] on both back panels.
  • Batch number format — replace [BATCH] with the format your printer wants (e.g. date-based like 240518, or sequential like B03).
  • EAN-13 barcode — buy real barcodes from GS1 UK (~£50 each for a starter licence); replace the placeholder bar on both back panels.
  • Nutrition values — send a sample of each SKU to a food-testing lab (or use an approved nutrition-calculation software). Once you have verified numbers, swap the "available on request" block for a proper table.
  • Allergen cross-check — confirm with your spice supplier that their garam masala and toasted cumin blends contain none of the 14 UK regulated allergens beyond mustard. Celery, sulphites and sesame sometimes appear in commercial blends.
  • Beef provenance — ask your butcher to confirm the beef is born, reared and slaughtered in the UK (all three steps), not just slaughtered in the UK, before keeping that line on-pack.
  • Halal chain-of-custody — keep records so you can evidence "made with halal beef" if challenged. Since you're not certified, this claim must be factually true and provable.
