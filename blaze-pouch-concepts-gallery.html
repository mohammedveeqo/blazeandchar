What to do once you've picked a direction

Tell me which of the 5 feels right (or which combination — e.g. "the typography of 01 with the spice scatter of 03 as a subtle background") and I'll ship the full system update in one pass:

1. Take your chosen concept and bake it into the canonical master, replacing the current design while keeping all the brand tokens, legal compliance, and system architecture. 2. Regenerate all 16 pair + shade variants with the new front design. 3. Update the supplier brief so the artwork reference reflects the new direction. 4. Update the website so the hero pouches match.

What won't change: back panel layout, nutrition structure, legal claims, halal-beef sourcing language, Wales heritage — all preserved. These four concepts only explore the front panel visual hierarchy.

Observations from your shelf research

Zero of the 11 jerky bags you photographed use a window — Jack Link's, Ember, Kings, The Curators, Cruga all chose a hero graphic (photograph, illustration, or typographic dominance) instead. All 4 new concepts follow this pattern.

Protein as a hero stat, not a cert — every bag on the shelf makes protein prominent (Kings "30% PROTEIN", Ember "20g per pack", Curators "13G NATURAL PROTEIN"). Concepts 01, 02, and 04 promote this. Concept 03 keeps it subtle to match its editorial lineage.

Every bag has provenance / heritage visible — Kings "PROUDLY MADE IN YORK", Cruga's savanna scene, Ember's Suffolk farm story. Concepts 01 and 04 foreground the Welsh heritage; 02 and 03 carry it more subtly via the Wales silhouette.
