How it's made

Three steps. No shortcuts.

Every batch is 18kg of beef, hand-rubbed, rested overnight, and dried for 9 hours. That's a batch a day, max. So when we sell out — we actually sell out.

01

Spice blend

Ground fresh · every batch

Whole Kashmiri chillies, cumin, coriander seed, amchoor, cassia, and smoked paprika toasted and ground the morning we marinate. Nothing sits around.

02

Slow marinate

14 hours · in tamarind & yoghurt

Premium silverside from a West Country farm gets a rub of yoghurt, tamarind paste, ginger and garlic. It rests cold overnight so the spice can go all the way in.

03

Slow dehydrate

9 hours · low & steady at 70°C

Dried on racks — not in a tumbler — so each strip keeps its texture. We pull the batch the moment it pulls cleanly but still tears. No further than that.

Ask us anything
Is it properly halal?
Yes — HMC-certified throughout. The beef comes from an HMC-approved West Country abattoir, and our East London kitchen is registered and inspected. Certification numbers are printed on the back of every pouch, and the full chain-of-custody PDF is available on request.
How spicy is it, really?
The Fire is medium-hot — think a good rogan josh, not a vindaloo. The Kashmiri chilli gives you colour and depth before it gives you heat. The Ember is warm rather than hot — the garam masala and cumin carry the flavour, and the chilli sits in the background. New to spice? Start with The Ember.
How long does an unopened pouch keep?
Four months from batch date, sealed, stored at room temperature out of direct sunlight. We date every pouch, and we ship within 72 hours of the seal going on, so you're getting at least 14 weeks of shelf life when it lands. Once opened, eat within 5 days (although in our experience a pouch rarely makes it past day two).
Why no soy? Everyone else uses soy.
Because soy sauce masks flavour. Most jerky uses it as a cheap umami shortcut and the result tastes like soy with meat in it. We build umami from the marinade itself — tamarind, amchoor, smoked paprika, slow marination — which is slower and costlier but means you actually taste the spice blend. It also means the jerky is suitable for people avoiding soy for allergy or dietary reasons.
When do you ship? How long does it take?
Orders placed before 2pm Monday–Thursday go out the same day. Friday/weekend orders ship Monday morning. Royal Mail 24 (tracked) in the UK, usually 1–2 working days, £1.55. International shipping to EU and US launching later this year — join the despatch list to be notified.
Why is it £5 for 50g? That's not cheap.
It isn't. 50g of finished jerky starts as about 160g of raw silverside — nearly 70% of the weight dries out. British halal silverside, 18-hour marinade, slow dehydrate, hand-packed in small batches means there's a floor to what this can cost to make properly. We'd rather charge £5 for a pouch you finish in one sitting than £3 for a pouch you don't finish at all.
Can I buy it in a shop?
Not yet — we're DTC-only while we build up batch capacity. A handful of independent London delis and farm shops carry it occasionally; if you're a buyer, email trade@blazeandchar.co.uk and we'll send you the wholesale deck.
