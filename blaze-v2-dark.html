More on the jerky

Slow-marinated.
Small-batch.
Halal-sourced.

We started Blaze & Char because nobody in the UK was making jerky that tasted like home.

The supermarket stuff is sweet, soy-soaked, and has never been anywhere near a spice. We wanted something that tasted like the kind of food we actually eat — Kashmiri chilli, tamarind, smoked paprika, garam masala. So we built it.

Every batch is marinated for 14 hours in a spice paste we mix by hand, then slow-dehydrated at 70°C until it's tender but still got bite. No teriyaki. No maple syrup. No "bold flavour" that means slightly more salt.

The beef is halal-sourced from a single British farm. We get it, we trim it, we spice it, we pack it. Small kitchen in East London, two people, one flavour obsession.

How it's made
01

Hand-mixed spice paste

Kashmiri chilli, tamarind, smoked paprika, ginger, garlic, black pepper. Ground fresh each week.

02

Slow marinate · 14 hours

Silverside slices, hand-trimmed, rubbed through the paste and rested overnight so the spice actually gets in.

03

Low & slow dehydrate

Dried at 70°C for 6 hours. Tender, not chewy leather. Resealable pouch, eat within 2 months.
