How it's made

Three steps. No shortcuts.

Every batch is small — a few kilos of beef at a time, hand-rubbed, rested, and slow-dried. One batch a day, max. So when we sell out — we actually sell out.

01

Spice blend

Ground fresh · every batch

Kashmiri chilli, cumin, smoked paprika, turmeric, kasuri methi and black cardamom — toasted and ground the morning we marinate. Nothing sits around.

02

Slow marinate

4–6 hours · tamarind, lemon & spice

British topside gets a wet marinade of tamarind water, tomato purée, lemon juice, apple cider vinegar, ginger and garlic. It rests cold for a few hours so the spice can go all the way in.

03

Slow dehydrate

9 hours · low & steady at 70°C

Dried on racks — not in a tumbler — so each strip keeps its texture. We pull the batch the moment it pulls cleanly but still tears. No further than that.

What we're serious about

Four things we won't compromise on.

Every British jerky on the shelf is either BBQ-sweet or peri-peri hot, made with reformed meat and shortcut spice mixes. We're making the opposite. Here's what that means in practice.

01 · The beef

Halal topside. Whole muscle, hand-cut.

Born, reared and slaughtered in the UK, bought from a local halal butcher — not a factory line. No reformed, pressed or injected meat. One cut (topside), sliced against the grain by hand so every strip bites the same way.

02 · The spice

Whole spices. Ground to order.

Kashmiri chilli, cumin, smoked paprika, turmeric, black cardamom, kasuri methi — whole, toasted fresh, ground the day of the batch. Plus tamarind water and lemon juice for the tang. No pre-mixed rubs, no anti-caking agents, no flavouring.

Ask us anything
Is it properly halal?
Yes — the beef is halal-sourced from our local halal butcher, and is born, reared and slaughtered in the UK. We are not HMC-certified yet, and we won’t claim we are until we pass their audit. What we can tell you is where the beef comes from and who handled it; chain-of-custody paperwork is available on request.
How spicy is it, really?
The Fire is medium-hot — think a good rogan josh, not a vindaloo. The Kashmiri chilli gives you colour and depth before it gives you heat. The Ember is warm rather than hot — the garam masala and cumin carry the flavour, and the chilli sits in the background. New to spice? Start with The Ember.
How long does an unopened pouch keep?
Each pouch is dated with a best-before and batch number, sealed, stored at room temperature out of direct sunlight. Exact shelf-life will be confirmed by food-lab test before the first pouch ships — we’ll print verified numbers on the pack, not invented ones. Once opened, eat within 5 days.
Why no soy? Everyone else uses soy.
Because soy sauce masks flavour. Most jerky uses it as a cheap umami shortcut and the result tastes like soy with meat in it. We build umami from the marinade itself — tamarind, tomato purée, smoked paprika, slow marination — which is slower and costlier but means you actually taste the spice blend. It also means the jerky is suitable for people avoiding soy for allergy or dietary reasons.
When do you ship? How long does it take?
Once we’re trading, orders placed before 2pm Monday–Thursday will go out the same day. Friday/weekend orders will ship Monday morning. Royal Mail tracked 48 in the UK, usually 1–2 working days, from £1.55. International shipping is on the roadmap once we’ve stress-tested UK fulfilment — join the despatch list to be notified.
Why is it £6 for 60g? That's not cheap.
It isn't. 60g of finished jerky starts as about 190g of raw topside — nearly 70% of the weight dries out. Halal British topside, a 4–6 hour marinade, slow dehydrate, hand-packed in small batches means there's a floor to what this can cost to make properly. We'd rather charge £6 for a proper portion you finish in one sitting than £3 for a nibble that leaves you hungry.
Can I buy it in a shop?
Not yet — we’ll launch direct-to-consumer first while we build batch capacity. Once we’re consistently selling out online we’ll approach independent delis and farm shops in Wales and beyond. If you’re a buyer who wants early access, email trade@blazeandchar.co.uk and we’ll add you to the wholesale list.
